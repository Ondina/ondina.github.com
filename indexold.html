Robot-Soccer
Intention: make a soccer-game using Robotlegs-framework, Robotlegs-Modular-Utility, Swiftsuspenders ChildInjectors, Robotlegs-StateMachine, as3-signals + signals-extensions-CommandSignal, (sharing a Server?)
Modules:

  1. RobotReferee->submodules->LeftEye+RightEye
  2. RobotPlayer ->submodules: LeftLeg + RightLeg
  3. RobotBall
  4. RobotTrainer

Roles

  1. RobotReferee : stopping, suspending or terminating the match,punish players(yellow-card, red-card , etc)
  2. RobotPlayer – kick ball, foul, kick adversary
  3. RobotBall – move
  4. RobotTrainer – load/unload Player

States

  1. RobotReferee : punishing_players, stopping_game, etc
  2. Robot_Player: moving, fouling, kicking, scoring,etc
  3. RobotBall: moving, not moving, goal,etc

Story:
Load:  RobotTrainer1, RobotTrainer2 and RobotReferee , RobotBall
RobotTrainer1 loads/unloads  RobotPlayer1, which loads/unloads his RightLeg and LeftLeg.(childinjectors???)
RobotTrainer2 loads/unloads  RobotPlayer2, which loads/unloads his RightLeg and LeftLeg.
RobotReferees starts the match. Now RobotPlayer1 and RobotPlayer2 can move their Legs.
Click on a Leg moves the Leg and kicks the RobotBall.
Each Module’s Model is responsible for settings pertaining to the Module. E.g. RobotPlayer1’s number of goals, penalties…
A Player’s info should  come  from a database: PlayersName,PlayersRole…when a Player is loaded into the game by his Trainer, and saved into a DB table at the end of the game or when the Player gets unloaded. The Game’s data has also a pendant on the backend .
